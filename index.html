The Life of Einstein

1879

Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire. His birth marked the beginning of the life of one of the most influential physicists in history.

1905

In 1905, Einstein published four groundbreaking papers in the Annalen der Physik, which revolutionized physics. These papers laid the foundation for modern physics and introduced concepts such as the special theory of relativity, the photoelectric effect, and the famous equation E=mc².

1915

In 1915, Einstein completed his general theory of relativity, which provided a new understanding of gravity. This theory proposed that gravity is not just a force between objects with mass, as described by Newtonian physics, but rather a curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.

1921

Einstein was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which was described in one of his 1905 papers. This achievement solidified his reputation as one of the greatest physicists of his time.

1933

With the rise of the Nazi Party in Germany, Einstein, who was of Jewish descent, emigrated to the United States in 1933. He settled in Princeton, New Jersey, where he continued his work in theoretical physics and became a professor at the Institute for Advanced Study.
